Membership Fees

Mosgiel Taekwon-Do is a nonprofit club and we have set our membership fee deliberately low to encourage people to get into Taekwon-Do and train with us. Costs are kept similar to previous years if possible, and we have a financial incentive for those who set up automatic payment provisions for payment of fees.

Note: Fees are payable in full every month, even if you do not attend some weeks as the cost of running the club remains the same. We train during school holidays also, and membership payments are required for the December/January break period. If you have difficulty paying, please get in touch so we can have a kōrero to figure out how best to deal with that situation.

National body fees

All students are required to be paid members of ITFNZ (our national body). This fee is set by the national body. Initial registration fees are required upon joining the organisation, or after returning from an absence of more than one year. Members must have a current membership prior to any gradings or events.

Individual Family (2+ students)
Registration Fee $70 $130

The initial registration will result in you getting an introductory pack, including an ITF manual (suitable for your level, e.g. mini-kids vs. main class) and some stickers etc. Subsequent yearly registrations are handled for you by the club for your convenience, so long as your account remains up to date with us. An account in arrears may result in being denied entry to gradings and tournaments.

Gradings

From time to time we have gup (coloured belt) gradings. These are run by the national body and are conducted by a registered examiner. Gradings are an official event where students demonstrate their knowledge and a successful grading will result in an increase in rank. Each member wishing to grade must pay the grading fee (usually through their own club) and be registered and paid-up on the national register before the grading event.

Note: mini-kid gradings are managed inside our own club and there are no grading fees for these. There may be a small cost to purchase a new belt though for some gradings. Please check with the mini-kids instructor about this. The schedule for mini-kid gradings is set by the club and as a general rule we try to do them a week or two after a gup grading.
